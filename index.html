Project Idea

Overview

My project is a web service that targets people from three countries, especially Chinese, Korean, and Japanese, and can buy and sell things that they no longer need. The products used in the daily lives of people in those three countries are very similar, and the style of buying and selling second-hand goods is also similar, so we can easily trade what we no longer need. The supported languages displayed will change according to each user, and a chat function will be provided so that negotiations such as the price of second-hand goods can be completed within the service. In addition to the usual second-hand sales, this service also supports product exchanges between users.

Motivation

In recent years, the number of Asians migrating to Australia, studying abroad, and working holidays has increased rapidly. Statistics from 2011 to 2020 show that 66,756 Chinese immigrated to Melbourne. There will be more than 260,000 Chinese students in 2019. More than 10,000 people from both Korea and Japan use working holiday visas every year. Products that East Asians often use in their daily lives are hard to come by in Australian stores. Therefore, many people buy and sell second-hand goods using group talk of chat apps such as WeChat, LINE, and cacao talk. There are many such groups in Melbourne (Australia). Most East Asians don't use Gumtree or eBay often. This service allows you to carry out Used sale that has been made with conventional chat apps through web services. Having one service makes it easier for users to find second-hand goods and makes it easier to get what they want. Second-hand sellers can promote their products to more users.
